About the Bachelor Thesis Research

The Bachelor Thesis Research (BTR) is the final academic component of the Maastricht Science Programme: a 30 ECTS, one-semester independent research project carried out under supervision of an experienced researcher. Students join a research group, design and execute their own work, and produce a written thesis and short video presentation.

Submissions made through this form are forwarded to the BTR office and used to build a curated list of opportunities for students. Listed projects are suggestions: students are always encouraged to also reach out to supervisors with their own ideas.

What makes a good BTR project
  • A clear, focused research question feasible in ~5 months
  • A realistic methodology with alternative approaches
  • Genuine learning opportunities for a bachelor student
  • Connection to current scientific literature
  • Brief consideration of ethical issues
BTR at a glance
30 ECTS · one full semester · independent research under supervision. Final deliverables: a written thesis (8,000–15,000 words) and a 5–7 minute video presentation.
Assessment & grading
Five components: Proposal (15%), Midterm practical (15%), Written thesis (25%), Final practical (35%), Video (10%). Pass mark: weighted average ≥ 5.5.
Where BTRs take place
Within Maastricht University, at other Dutch institutions, or at international universities and research centres. External hosts require an Internal Advisor from MSP/UM.
Roles in a BTR
Research Supervisor — guides and assesses the student. Internal Advisor — safeguards academic standards. BTR Coordinator & Administrator — oversee the programme.
A note for students
Listed projects are suggestions, not the full set of opportunities. You are always encouraged to reach out to supervisors directly with your own ideas.
